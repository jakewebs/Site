About This Project

Language: Python

The idea of training a computer algorithm to assist in appraising stock prices is hardly new, and is what I attempted along with two others this summer. Specifically, we used Support Vector Machines as our classifier to distinguish between poor performers and strong performers. We sought to mix short-term data (such as momentum and volatitly in stock prices), with medium-to-long-term data about the company (such as info and trends from 10-Q and 10-K reports).

Ultimately, we did see some success with the network, outperforming the Efficient Market Hypothesis (which would expect movement to be somewhat random, or 50/50 accuracy), but not by a statistically significant margin. Nonetheless, this provided an interesting synthesis of business and technical knowledge and taught me a good amount about web-scraping and how machine learning works.


My Contributions

  • Researching financial metrics to consider, along with the specific machine learning framework.
  • Using BeautifulSoup to access and scrape financial reports from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on a number of the most valuable companies in the S&P 500 over a period of several years.
  • Calculating key financial metrics using data extracted as previously mentioned and writing to and organized CSV so that it could be considered by our classification algorithm.
